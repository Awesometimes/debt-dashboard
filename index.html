Borrower paths

  • Set automatic monthly payments with a fixed due date.
  • Make a partial lump-sum payment from savings, tax refund, or bonus.
  • Ask family for short-term help and pay them back on a clear schedule.
  • Compare bank or credit-union personal loan rates before borrowing.
  • Sell unused items or take temporary extra work until the balance is smaller.

Lender paths

  • Ask for a signed repayment agreement with payment amount, due date, and default terms.
  • Move every payment through traceable methods and keep receipts.
  • Send a calm written demand that gives a specific deadline.
  • Offer one negotiated discount only if it brings immediate money in.
  • Consider mediation, small claims, or a lawyer if the agreement keeps slipping.

Massachusetts to Peru

Cross-border reality check

A borrower in Lima makes this less about proving the debt and more about jurisdiction, valid service, and whether a judgment can actually be collected.

  • Massachusetts small claims is generally capped at $7,000, so $20,000 may require a regular civil case or waiving part of the claim.
  • Massachusetts contract claims are generally time-limited; ask a lawyer whether recent payments or written acknowledgments change the clock.
  • Serving court papers in Peru can require international service rules, translations, and a local lawyer or formal request process.
  • A Massachusetts judgment may be most useful if he has U.S. assets, U.S. income, or bank accounts you can legally reach.
  • If collection would need to happen in Peru, get advice from counsel familiar with Peruvian enforcement before spending money filing.
This app is not legal or financial advice. For a debt this old, check your state statute of limitations before threatening suit or accepting a token payment.
